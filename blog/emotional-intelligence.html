EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence (EQ) is our ability to be aware of our own and others emotional state. Our thoughts and actions are under constant influence from emotions connected to our past, present, and future, it’s a part of being human. They are a force to be reckoned with and it can be hard finding a balance between letting our emotions control our lives and shutting them out completely. Good EQ is dependent on our ability to perceive, reason, understand, and manage emotions so they can guide our thoughts and actions to connect better with ourselves and others.

How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ is dependent on self-awareness, self-regulation, social awareness, and relationship management. EQ isn’t static, it can change overtime through practice and learning from experiences. IQ is more of a fixed metric and is hard to improve. It is measured through our capability in intellectual pursuits such as logical reasoning, word comprehension, and math skills.

In short EQ (Emotional Quotient) indicates a person’s ability to understand the emotions of themselves and others. On the other hand IQ (Intelligence Quotient) indicates a person’s intelligence level. It represents someone's ability to use information and logic to solve problems and understand or communicate complex ideas.

Why is EQ important?

Having a high level of emotional intelligence allows us to empathize with others, communicate effectively, and be both self and socially aware. Naturally, being good at these skills will help us to build good relationships, improve leadership, reduce stress (personally and in team environments) and manage conflict. Ultimately improving and working on EQ can bring us happiness through job satisfaction, meaningful relationships and personal achievement to name a few.
