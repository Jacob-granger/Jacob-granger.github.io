MY LEARNING PLAN FOR BECOMING A WEB DEVELOPER

My Learning Plan for Bootcamp

Having completed foundations I am now excited to enter Bootcamp and apply and build on my skill set in a classroom based setting.

Revisiting my long term vision as a web developer I identified that the most important component of this goal for me, is to be using my skills to contribute to something that will ultimately add value to the lives of others.

A strength I can bring to Bootcamp is my ability to empathize and relate to a broad range of people. I think this will help productively during peer coding sessions and group projects. I also think it will be valuable for enhancing my social experience during Bootcamp and ultimately create friendships within my cohort. I also have discovered that naturally I bring a growth mindset to my learning that will help maximize my learning experience across all aspects of Bootcamp.

Foundations has highlighted that I need to work on knowing when I should ask for help. I feel Bootcamp will provide a good opportunity for me to work on this limitation as I think being in a live classroom setting will encourage me to reach out for help more frequently. The biggest non technical challenge for me is going to be adhering to my Te Whare Tapa Whā wellbeing plan. I have noticed when I become overwhelmed and stressed, I have a tendency to neglect self care, highlighting the need for increased discipline in consistently prioritizing and allocating time for.

During Bootcamp I would like to see myself improve on my reflective skills. I would like to work on my ability to reflect without requiring prompts from external sources. While I have enjoyed this aspect of foundations, I would like to develop a more independent ability to reflect on my learning journey.

I aim to create a more precise day to day structure that establishes how I will use my time outside of scheduled class time to ensure optimal productivity and well being. This plan will allocate specific time slots for focused studying, practicing exercises, and collaborating with my peers, while also incorporating breaks for exercise, relaxation, and reflection to help maintain a healthy mindset. I will find time at the end of each day to revise and make any necessary changes for the following day.

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

A long term goal of mine is to one day be working on healthcare systems here in Aotearoa/New Zealand. This would be a meaningful opportunity for me to contribute to an industry that would benefit from innovative change, and could directly improve the lives of others. I want to build on my skill set not only as a developer but as a designer too. Although my primary goal is to become a full stack web developer, I would like to supplement this skill-set with an education in user experience design. I think having an understanding of both fields will strongly enhance my versatility and adaptability within a software development team.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

My analytical approach to independent learning allows me to break down large complex concepts into smaller more digestible sections to help understand and retain abstract material. Another strength of mine is persistence. I don’t easily give up. The less I understand something the more drive I have to gain a comprehensive understanding of it. In group learning environments I think my biggest strength is being able to empathize well with others. This will help me form good relationships, and communicate effectively with my peers.

A limitation I encounter often is identifying when I have spent too much time trying to solve or understand something. I easily lose track of time when persisting and can easily abandon any learning strategy or plan I have in place. Knowing my limits and asking for external help sooner is definitely something I need to work on and hope to improve during this course.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to work on utilizing the expertise of my peers more frequently to ask for critique and feedback on my work. I often wait until I have completed a task before sharing it and I think I would benefit a lot from seeking input from others more regularly throughout my work flow.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme

- At the beginning of each week I will outline the overall objectives that need completing by the end of the week. I will then break these down into smaller daily goals with specified time frames.

- At the end of each day I will dedicate time to reflect and assess whether I have achieved my goals for the day. During this time I can make any adjustments to my learning plan for the rest of the week if necessary.

- Currently I am using the pomodoro technique for time management and have been starting and finishing each day at consistent times.

- I will include time within my daily schedule to allow for breaks away from studying. If I feel that stress is building and I am struggling to keep on top of my workload I will be proactive at getting in touch with my facilitator before it becomes unmanageable.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

My current strategy for overcoming any learning barriers I encounter is to first establish the root cause of the issue. Next is to define a reasonable period of time to work on the problem independently through research, course notes, discord, and other resources etc. If I am unable to progress, and the task isn’t urgent, I’ll switch to working on something else unrelated or take a short break away from work and return to the problem refreshed, ready for another attempt. At this point if I have been unsuccessful I will reach out to my course facilitator or home group and ask for help.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

My expectation is that during any meetings or review sessions with my facilitator, they're as honest as they can possibly be with any feedback regarding how I'm tracking during this course. Although I will be committed to working on and improving my learning plan as much as possible myself, I hope that my facilitator will bring up any concerns, suggestions, or advice as they become aware of my learning style.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things

Currently I have no extracurricular obligations. However it is important for me to formulate a learning plan that allows for time to add non-course related hobbie/activities either in the evenings or on the weekends. I have just moved to a new location in the country so naturally this is a component of my learning plan that will evolve as I progress through the course.
