Neuroplasticity + Growth Mindset

How does understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefit people?

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity teaches us that our brain has the capacity to change and grow. In short, it explains how our brain can continuously change and reorganize itself throughout our life, in response to internal and external stimulation. It is important to note that our brain has this ability regardless of our age and challenges traditional beliefs that our brains become fixed and unchangeable past a certain age. By understanding the principles of neuroplasticity we can make significant improvements in learning, rehabilitation, mental health and personal growth.

How can I engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for my benefit?

Having an understanding of neuroplasticity motivates me to consistently build and work on areas of my life in which I perceive myself as average or lacking confidence. It has taught me that not only can I keep improving on the skills I already have, but I can also acquire entirely new skills no matter my age. I am excited to apply this knowledge to both my studies and relationships, in pursuit of personal growth and meaningful connections with others.

What are some ways for me to increase my neuroplasticity?

An important way for me to increase neuroplasticity currently is through practicing and learning new skills. This can help stimulate the formation of new neural pathways and also strengthen existing ones. Additionally, I would like to increase neuroplasticity through practicing mindfulness and meditation. Studies show that this can help increase attention, reduce stress and ultimately will facilitate my ability to apply myself to learning more effectively.

What is Growth Mindest and why is it relevant?

Neuroplasticity and “Growth Mindset” go hand in hand. Neuroplactity explains the science behind how our brains can adapt and change, whereas a growth mindset is more of a belief system that supports the idea that our capabilities, both intellectually and physically, are not fixed traits and rather they can be worked on and improved. A person who has adopted a growth mindset is aware that they can develop and improve not only from effort and practice, but also from embracing challenges and seeing them as opportunities rather than setbacks. Ultimately having a growth mindset promotes a love of learning, no matter the trials and tribulations we encounter along the way.
