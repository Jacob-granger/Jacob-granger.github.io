FOUNDATIONS REFLECTION

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I think the first thing I have learnt about myself is related to my willingness to ask for help. This has been a quality of mine that I have worked on in the past and have made a lot of progress with in certain environments (previous jobs etc). However I haven't studied for a long time and reflecting on this quality recently whilst studying again, has revealed I need to work on asking for help more frequently within a learning based environment.

I can be reluctant to put my work out for others to review when I feel like it isn't in a complete state that’s up to my own personal standards. This is definitely an ego thing and when I think about it I haven't been in many situations where I have had to share work of mine that's in a WIP state.

Understanding the principles of growth mindset has taught me alot about my approach to learning. An aspect that I found really interesting was considering that we can adopt different mindsets based on different contexts/environments we can find ourselves in. It’s made me really think about different situations and the type of mindset I bring to each of them and that it's important to work in them individually rather than all together at once.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

When learning, having a clear understanding of what our core values are will ultimately provide the foundations for our goals and objectives, and provide direction for what we aim to achieve during our learning journey. When programming they can act as a guide for ethical and responsible development practices.

Empathy is essential for being able to understand how we connect with others within our learning environment. Having empathy for the people around us can enrich our learning experience for ourselves and for others, fostering a healthy learning environment. When programming, having empathy can allow us to work more effectively within development teams and can facilitate a more user-centered approach to programming. This can result in building more meaningful systems that end users are more likely to adopt and use.

Having self awareness when learning allows us to understand our strengths and weaknesses. With this awareness we can apply our strengths more effectively and identify areas that require improvement. When programming, having self awareness can help us understand our skill level, areas of expertise and limitations. It can help with setting realistic goals and provide us with valuable insight as to when we need to ask for help and utilize the expertise of others to improve productivity within a team.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

What surprised me was realizing how little time I spend trying to improve my “human” skills. It has made me realize that if I want to improve these skills I need to dedicate time and effort to practice the same way I do with technical skills.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I found it challenging to apply and practise these skills throughout foundations as there was an overwhelming amount of content introduced all at once. However, I think entering bootcamp will provide me with more opportunities to practice and develop these new skills I have learnt.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Having previously studied a degree in IT I felt there was a lack of teaching surrounding “soft skills” that are essential within the industry. EDA has recognised the importance of equipping graduates with a strong skill set in both “soft” and “hard” skills, if they are to hit the ground running as a web developer and excel within their roles. At the end of the day most of the roles we find ourselves in require working with people. Our actions affect others (outputs) and others actions affect ourselves (inputs). Learning how to manage these inputs and outputs is essential for succeeding within the IT industry.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead?

Definitely not! On the whole I have found the core learning material insightful and valuable and I look forward to applying these new skills in a team based scenario and independently. Like with getting better at any technical skill we can only develop and improve our human skills through expanding our understanding and practicing.
