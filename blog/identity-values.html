EXPLORING MY IDENTITY AND VALUES

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now

An interesting situation that would arise during my role as production manager in my last job, was when a colleague would make an error within their role which subsequently would cause major irreversible issues further down the production life cycle. As production manager my employer would consult with me to understand what went wrong and essentially look to discover who made the error and why, in order to prevent the issue from recurring. In a particular incident my employer confronted me about an error that had caused major issues. During this encounter they had already decided who they thought was responsible and put the blame on them and left it to me to resolve the issue. However, in this situation I realized it was me who was responsible for making the error. I could have easily said nothing and allowed someone else to take the blame. Instead I made the ethical decision to tell my boss that it was me who had messed up relieving the blame from my colleague.

Although I would consider this example to be a minor ethical decision. I believe it is an example that helps me understand some of my values and reinforces who I am as a person. Looking at the different outcomes based on my decision the consequences are very minor. However, often it’s the smaller decisions with minor outcomes where people decide to forgo their moral principles as it’s easy to think “well it doesn’t really matter nothing bad will come of this”. Being honest and having compassion for others are core values of mine. During this situation I could have abused my position of responsibility and let someone else take the blame who didn't have the chance to stand up for themselves. Reflecting on this occasion reinforces to me that compassion for others and honesty are values that I strongly adhere to regardless of the situation. Of course, although not easy at the time, I still believe that this was the right decision to make. I think having empathy for others and remaining as honest as you possibly can in all situations promotes trust, and is essential for forming healthy relationships with others.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

The biggest influence on who I am today has come from my parents and my upbringing. Although there wasn’t a strong sense of tradition within our household growing up there was a large presence of core family values that we all share and I still value immensely today. Having grown up on an off-grid lifestyle block I have always had a strong connection to nature and an understanding of the importance of sustainable living. One of the values that this lifestyle helped shape in me is how to be self- sufficient. It has taught me to adapt quickly in challenging environments and to take responsibility to initiate change, rather than relying on external help to make it happen for me. The other value this upbringing has taught me is contentment. This lifestyle has helped shape the concept of not wanting more than I need. Living a more simplistic life has taught me to identify and value what’s essential in my life rather than seeking happiness through what I don’t have.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey

A quality of mine that I’m most proud of are my interpersonal skills. Being able to empathize and communicate easily with a broad range of people has helped me thrive in team environments, manage conflict, and build good meaningful relationships amongst friends and co-workers. Listening, understanding, and being patient with people will allow me to learn from others around me and foster good communication and collaboration amongst my peers.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

One of my limitations is seeking help. Often, when completing a task if I get to a point that is difficult I can find it challenging to admit to myself that I need external help. This has been a moderately consistent barrier in my past. Ultimately this could affect my career development journey by leading me to become unnecessarily stressed and stuck during tasks, which would prevent me from moving forward quickly. This in turn would also affect team members/ co-workers etc.

Another limitation to my learning and career development has been procrastination. This involves starting and completing tasks. This means I can feel disappointed in the quality of work I have produced. I would say this has been an ongoing, minor obstacle throughout my learning journey. Becoming aware of this limitation, and identifying strategies to improve this has been a major focus of mine. I think as I progress through my learning journey and gain more experience and opportunities to deal with this barrier, it will become less and less of a limitation.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why

An example that first comes to mind was when I worked in a group with two other members. During team meetings there was often conflict between the other two as they knew each other well outside of the team. I struggled to weigh in on the decision making and express my view point as it felt like I was choosing a side when I expressed my opinion.

Initially with these meetings I would stay silent when conflict between them arose. By not contributing it made the meetings unproductive and made my presence feel redundant. To try and facilitate productivity, I decided that in the end I had to push aside the fear of how my opinion would affect their egos and focus on what the objective of the meetings were. Rather than force my view into the mix of already conflicted opinions, I would first discuss each option by considering the pros and cons of each of their suggestions and I would keep a strong focus on the problem at hand. This would help neutralize the conflict as it would divert the discussion away from “who is right and who is wrong,” and back to the main objective, allowing me to voice my own opinion on the matter without feeling like I was picking a side.

If I were in this situation again I think my strategy would be to let them know how their conflict affected my contribution at group meetings. This may have given them the opportunity to gain awareness of the situation, and aim to change, in order to make the meetings more productive.
