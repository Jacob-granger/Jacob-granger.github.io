INLINE VS INLINE BLOCK, WHAT'S THE DIFFERENCE?

Learning the basics of becoming a great web developer can be tricky and confusing. A simple concept that’s important to grasp is the display properties of block, inline, and inline-block elements. Having a solid understanding of this will assist you tremendously in your journey to creating beautiful layouts in no time. So without further ado, let's dive into it!

DISPLAY: BLOCK


First let’s describe our elements such as paragraphs and headings as boxes. Our first type of box has a display type “Block”. A Block box is greedy and takes up an entire line on our screen and doesn't share it with other boxes. If other boxes want to join they have to find space either above or below a “block” box. They will only stack on top of each other, not beside each other. How much space a block box takes up can be defined by its dimension, margin, and padding properties.
